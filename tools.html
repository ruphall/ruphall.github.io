Cyber Security Overview

What is Cyber Security?

Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. These attacks can be aimed at stealing, altering, or destroying sensitive data or interrupting digital operations.

Why Do We Need Cyber Security?

  • To protect confidential and sensitive data from theft
  • To secure financial transactions and business integrity
  • To ensure the availability of essential digital services
  • To comply with privacy and data protection regulations
  • To prevent cyberattacks and reduce risks of damage

Cyber Attack Methods

  • Phishing: Deceiving users to steal login credentials
  • Ransomware: Encrypting files and demanding payment
  • SQL Injection: Inserting malicious queries in databases
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MitM): Eavesdropping on communication
  • DDoS: Overloading servers to shut down services
  • Zero-Day Exploit: Exploiting unknown vulnerabilities

Common Hacker Tools

  • Metasploit: Exploit framework for penetration testing
  • Nmap: Network scanning and host discovery
  • Wireshark: Packet capturing and analysis
  • Hydra: Brute force login cracking tool
  • John the Ripper: Password cracking tool

Cyber Security Prevention Methods

  • Use strong and unique passwords with multi-factor authentication
  • Keep software, OS, and apps up-to-date
  • Install antivirus and anti-malware protection
  • Enable firewalls and IDS/IPS systems
  • Encrypt sensitive communications and data
  • Limit user access with least privilege principles
  • Educate users with security awareness training

Cyber Security Fundamentals

  • Risk Management: Identify, analyze, and mitigate security risks
  • Access Control: Restrict system and data access to authorized users only
  • Incident Response: Detect, contain, and recover from attacks
  • Security Policies: Define acceptable use and safe behavior standards
  • Regular Monitoring: Continuous tracking of systems and logs

CIA Triad

The CIA Triad represents three core principles of information security:

  • Confidentiality: Preventing unauthorized access to sensitive data
  • Integrity: Ensuring data is accurate and unmodified
  • Availability: Ensuring systems and services are accessible when needed

Attack Surface Area

The Attack Surface is the total of all possible points where an attacker can try to enter or extract data from a system.

Examples:

  • Unpatched software or OS vulnerabilities
  • Exposed APIs or web services
  • Open ports and network services
  • Unsecured Wi-Fi or IoT devices
  • Phishing emails or social engineering paths
  • Misconfigured cloud environments

Goal: Reduce the attack surface to minimize risk.
